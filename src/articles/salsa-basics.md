Salsa is one of the most joyful and social dances you can learn. You don't need
a partner, prior experience, or fancy shoes — just a willingness to move.

## The basic step

Salsa is danced over eight counts of music, but you only step on six of them:

1. **Count 1** — step forward with your left foot.
2. **Count 2** — rock your weight back onto your right foot.
3. **Count 3** — bring your left foot back to centre.
4. **Count 4** — pause (this is the tricky bit for beginners!).
5. **Counts 5–7** — mirror the pattern, stepping back with the right foot.
6. **Count 8** — pause again.

> Tip: keep your steps small and your knees soft. Salsa lives in the hips, not
> the feet.

## What to practise this week

- Count out loud while you step — it locks the rhythm into your body.
- Practise the basic step in front of a mirror for five minutes a day.
- Listen to salsa music and try to find the "1" in the beat.
